Lesson 1.1: Cells

Before we can create and explore cellular automata like the ones seen in the introduction, we must establish some key concepts that will help to build skills needed to work with more exciting, complex automata.

The core building blocks of cellular automata are cells. At any given point in time, these cells have a finite (or fixed) number of states that they can take. Most commonly, cellular automata have states of either on or off (sometimes referred to as alive or dead), though more complex automata can take more.
