Lesson 1.3: Ruleset Exploration

The ruleset you created in the last section was rule 30, but you can make many other rulesets for cellular automata. But why is this rule #30?

The naming convention for rule numbers was created by Stephen Wolfram and is known as Wolfram Code. Under this scheme, each rule is defined by a set of configurations with a particular cell’s neighbor, just like you saw at the end of the last section.

The specific number assigned to a ruleset comes from the binary representation of on and off states. For example, 000111102 is binary for 30, which is the ruleset used in the previous section. Toggle each button to explore the generation of rule numbers further below:
